## Executive Summary

Bartlett is a ~60,000-person suburb of Memphis with a dense corridor of small businesses along Stage Road, Bartlett Road, and Germantown Parkway. A significant portion of these businesses — especially trades, personal care, and auto services — operate with **no website at all**, relying entirely on Facebook pages, Yelp listings, or outdated Yellow Pages entries. Their better-funded competitors in the Memphis metro have modern mobile sites, online booking, and strong local SEO — making these Bartlett operators invisible to the 85%+ of consumers who search Google before visiting a local business.

**Your pitch in one sentence:** "Your competitor three miles away is showing up at the top of Google and taking your customers — I can fix that in two weeks."

## Cold-Call Talking Points (Universal)

Use these across all pitches:

1. **The Google test close:** "Pull up Google Maps right now and search '[your business type] Bartlett TN' — do you see your business?" (Most won't.)

2. **The competitor anchor:** Name a specific local competitor who DOES have a site and is ranking above them. Show it on your phone.

3. **The cost-per-lead math:** "If your site brings in one new customer a month, what's that worth to you? For auto repair, that's $200–$500. The site pays for itself in 60 days."

4. **The urgency hook (nail salons):** "I'm talking to three nail salons in Bartlett this week. Whoever I build the site for first will own Google for this category — the others will be playing catch-up."

5. **The POC close:** "I already built a mock-up of what your site could look like — can I show you? No obligation." *(This is where your Claude Code POC comes in.)*
